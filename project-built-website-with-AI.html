ZERO Coding Experience, No Paid Platforms:
How I Built My Portfolio Website from Scratch (With AI!)

Having a portfolio website is essential for any designer. But let’s be honest—how many of us neglect it once it’s up and running? That was me. I had my website on a free plan with a platform and barely updated it. My clients were happy, so I just let it be.
Then one day, I logged in, and surprise—the platform was no longer free. Not only that, I couldn’t make changes to my website anymore.
When I checked my dashboard, my heart sank. They wanted 200 euros a year just to keep my site live. That’s when I realized I needed another solution—a cheaper one. I didn’t need anything fancy, just a simple site to showcase my projects. But how could I do it?

The AI Solution
I turned to AI for help. “How can I build my own website without spending a fortune?” I asked.
AI suggested using another website builder. But the problem? These “cheaper” options were still 100+ euros a year. Nope, not for me. I wanted something closer to 10 euros a year, with a personal domain and basic functionality.
That’s when I had a wild idea: what if I built it myself? I had no experience coding, but AI had been making headlines for doing everything. Maybe, just maybe, I could make it work.

My Coding Adventure
I started by asking AI to help me create a basic portfolio. I wanted a layout where clicking an image opened a content page. Easy, right? Well, not exactly.
AI sent back a wall of code—lines of red and green that made no sense to me. What was I supposed to do? I needed a code editor (I chose Atom) and a browser (Chrome). With those tools in hand, I copy-pasted the code and crossed my fingers.
It worked... sort of. The layout appeared, but it wasn’t quite what I wanted. That’s when the real work began.

Here’s how my workflow looked:
1. Ask AI a question or share a rough layout idea.
2. Receive a block of code with explanations.
3. Copy-paste it into Atom and test it in Chrome.
4. Make adjustments when things didn’t look right.
This process repeated dozens of times. Sometimes, I got stuck on silly things, like centering text. Other times, I had to describe my problem more clearly for AI to understand. I even tried asking different AI models when one didn’t give me the answer I needed. Eventually, I realized that to fix certain issues, I needed to understand the code myself. Slowly but surely, margins, font sizes, and colors started to make sense. It was like learning a new language—but with an incredibly patient AI tutor. After a week of trial and error, I had a layout I loved. And guess what? I felt like half a coder!

The Multilingual Challenge
Next, I decided to make my website multilingual—English, French, and Chinese. That’s three versions of every page.
Many platforms offer tools to simplify this, but since I was coding manually, I had to create and link each version myself. For every French project page, I had to ensure the menu linked back to the French main page—not the English one.
It was tedious. I had 12 projects to showcase, meaning I had to check and fix the menu links 36 times.
Thankfully, AI was a lifesaver for the translations. I simply asked, “Translate this into French and Chinese,” and within a day, all my text was ready.

SEO: The Necessary Evil
Search Engine Optimization (SEO) was next. It’s not glamorous, but it’s crucial if you want people to find your website.
AI broke it down step by step, making it manageable. I added language tags to every page, generated SEO-friendly titles and descriptions, and even renamed my image and video files to improve search rankings.
Honestly, I’ve never used such long file names before, but if it helps with SEO, I’ll take it!

Hosting for Free
Finally, I had to get my website online. At first, I tried hosting it on someone else’s server. While it worked, it wasn’t secure—no SSL! After days of frustration and even contacting the server’s support team, I gave up.
I turned back to AI: “Where can I host my website for free?” The answer: GitHub. At first, I was intimidated. GitHub felt like a space for super-smart developers. But after everything I’d learned, I wasn’t afraid to dive in. With AI guiding me step by step, I uploaded my files, and voilà—my portfolio was live!

Final Touches
To track my site’s performance, I added Google Analytics. AI provided a simple guide, making the setup a breeze.

What I Learned
With AI, I realized I could learn almost anything. You don’t need prior experience—just motivation and patience. AI was like having a personal tutor, answering every question without judgment.

AI tools I used:
• ChatGPT (free version) • Gemini • Doubao (for translating Chinese descriptions—it was more flexible than ChatGPT).
Thinking about building your own website? Go for it! If I can do it, so can you.

Why This Approach Worked for Me
While it wasn’t the easiest route, building my website from scratch taught me so much. And it didn’t cost a fortune! If you’re a designer or creative who wants more control over your portfolio, this journey might be for you too.
